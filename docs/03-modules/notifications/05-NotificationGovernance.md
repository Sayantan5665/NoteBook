# 05 — Notification Governance

> **Module:** Notifications
> **Status:** Approved
> **Applies To:** Notebook Application

---

## 1. Purpose

The Governance document defines the strict boundaries of the Notifications module, ensuring it remains an isolated presentation coordinator rather than a business logic orchestrator.

---

## 2. Ownership Boundaries

### 2.1 Notifications Owns:
- **Notification coordination:** Managing the queue and lifecycle.
- **Delivery coordination:** Routing to specific channels.
- **User notification lifecycle:** Tracking state from creation to dismissal.

### 2.2 Notifications Does NOT Own:
- **Notes**
- **Editor**
- **AI**
- **Search**
- **Sync**
- **Backup**
- **Todos**
- **Settings**

---

## 3. Consistency Rules

- **Notifications communicate events generated by other modules.**
- **They never execute business operations.**
- **No Domain Leakage:** The Notification module does not know what a `Note` or an `Attachment` is. It only knows about `title`, `message`, and `severity`.

---

## 4. Business Rules

- **Notifications communicate information to users.**
- **Notifications never own business logic.**
- **Notifications are ephemeral communication artifacts.** Future versions may optionally provide notification history, but notifications never become Notebook entities.
- **Notebook entities remain the canonical source of truth.**
- **Infrastructure modules coordinate processing.**
- **Derived artifacts never replace Notebook entities.**
- **Ownership never transfers.**

---

## 5. Acceptance Criteria

- Code reviews confirm the Notification module does not inject or depend on `NoteService` or `SyncService`.
